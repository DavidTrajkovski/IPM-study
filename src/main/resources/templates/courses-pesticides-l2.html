Lection 2: Pesticide Toxicity

Toxicity is a measure of the capacity of the pesticide to cause injury.

Hazard is the potential for a chemical to cause injury.

Routes of Entry – Three routes by which a pesticide can enter human body are:

  • Dermal – Occurs when the skin or the eyes are exposed to chemicals.
  • Inhalation – The pesticide enter through nose and mouth in vapours, gaseous or small droplets state.
  • Oral – The pesticide enter through mouth by swallowing or splashing of the chemicals.

Determining Toxicity – The toxicity of a particular pesticide is determined by subjecting test animals to different dosages of an active ingredient and to each of its formulated products.

Acute Toxicity – Is the capacity of a pesticide to cause injury from a single exposure and is expressed as LD50. LD50 is the amount of toxicant required to kill 50 percent of testing population under a standard set of conditions. LD50 is used to compare toxicity of different active ingredients as well as different formulation of the same active ingredient.

Signal words – All pesticide must carry signal words on the label:

a)

DANGER, POISON + Skull and Crossbones

indicate high toxicity (category I) of the pesticide

Also Spanish word PELIGRO must appear on the label.

b)

DANGER no skull and crossbones

this symbol occurs when possible skin irritation and eye effects are more severe than suggested by the LD50.

c)

WARNING

indicate moderate toxicity (category II) of the pesticide

Spanish word AVISO must be present also on the label.

d)

CAUTION

Insecticide and Pesticide Groups

a) Organophosphates and Carbamates

Insecticide of most concern which inhibit (reduce, slowdown) cholinesterase, a chemical critical for normal function of the nervous system. Absences of symptoms more than 12 hours after exposure generally exclude organophosphate and carbamate poisoning. - Symptoms of this poisoning depend of the amount touched, inhaled or ingested. They can be Mild Symptoms (headache, weakness, anxiety, tremors, and blurred vision), Moderate Symptoms (nausea, salivation, vomiting, and sweating, muscular twitching), Severe Symptoms (diarrhea, breathing difficulty, convulsion, and coma).

b) Chlorinated Hydrocarbons

Some of these chemicals alter ion permeability in the nervous system. Symptoms can be Vomiting, Trembling or shaking and Muscular weakness.

c) Pyrethrum and Pyrethroids

These chemicals alter ion permeability in the nervous system. Symptoms of poisoning are Contact dermatitis, Asthmatic reaction and other allergic reaction.

d) Petroleum Products

High volatility produces coughing and nausea. Symptoms are Nausea, Coughing, Vomiting, Weakness, Slow and shallow breathing.

e) Inorganics (Sulfur, Arsenic)

Heavy metals bind enzyme proteins on the cellular lever and cause inflammation of the gastrointestinal tract endothelium. Symptoms of poisoning are vomiting, bloody diarrhea, headache, chills and dizziness.

f) Herbicides and Fungicides

Herbicide and fungicide are designed as plant poisons. Level of poisoning varies with the herbicide or fungicide, the amount absorbed and individual health. Dermal Contact (skin irritation, discoloration and itching); Inhalation (burning sinuses, coughing, hoarseness and upper respiratory congestion); Ingestion (throat irritation, chest pain, nausea, diarrhea, muscle twitching, sweating, headache and weakness)
